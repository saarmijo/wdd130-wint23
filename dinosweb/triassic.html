Triassic

The Triassic period was 252-201 million years ago. This period is also known as the ‘Age of Reptiles.’ During this time on Earth, all of the continents were squished together in one super continent called Pangea. Dinosaurs during the Triassic period were a lot smaller than the ones we see in movies now, but they were the start of something big. Some popular dinosaurs from this period include: Pantydracos, Saturnalias, Daemonosaurus, and Eoraptors.

Pantydraco

Pantydraco’s name comes from the Welsh word Pant-y-ffynnon, which means well / spring, and draco, which means dragon.

Where found: Bonvilston in South Wales, UK

Estimated length: 3 m (10 ft.)

Saturnalia

One of the earliest known dinosaurs, as well as one of the earliest known ‘plant eating’ dinosaurs.

Where found: Santa Maria Formation, southern Brazil; Pebbly Arkose Formation, Zimbabwe

Estimated length: 1.5 m (5 ft.)

Daemonosaurus

This unusual-looking dinosaur had large eyes and a short, blunt snout with protruding teeth.

Where found: Ghost Ranch, Chinle Formation, New Mexico

Estimated length: 1.5-2.2 m (5-7 ft.)

Eoraptor

This was one of the first ever dinosaurs to have evolved

Where found: Argentina

Estimated length: 1 m (3.25 ft.)
