Jurassic

The Jurassic period was 201.3â€“145.0 million years ago. During this period, the supercontinent Pangea split apart. Dinosaurs during this period begin to get bigger. This was a golden time for dinosaurs. Some popular dinosaurs from this time period include: Brachiosaurus, Megalosaurus, Archaeopteryx, and Stegosaurus.

Brachiosaurus

With a long neck and tail, and an enormous body supported by four tree-trunk-like legs, is a true giant.

Where found: Colorado, USA

Estimated length: 10-21 m (59-69 ft.)

Megalosaurus

First ever dinosaur to be named. It walked on its hind legs, and used its tail as a counterbalance.

Where found: England, possibly France

Estimated length: 9 m (30 ft.)

Archaeopteryx

Scientists are unsure whether this dinosaur was capable of true flapping flight, or sophisticated gliding.

Where found: Bavaria, southern Germany

Estimated length: 0.5 m (20 in.)

Stegosaurus

With forelegs longer than its hindlegs, this plant eater had a downward slant. The most distinctive feature of the Stegosaurus is the two rows of large plates that run along its back.

Where found: Morrison Formation, western USA

Estimated length: 9 m (30 ft.)
